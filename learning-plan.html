In order to work and lead the group to success, I would need work on expressing my feelings and opinions to others as well as listening and understanding theirs. I would want to expect the Bootcamp team to communicate regularly and be available to answer my questions and address the issue. So far in my learning journey at Dev Academy, the team, the facilitators were all very supportive so I have no problem on the Bootcamp as well.

Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te whare tapa whā is a model of health that represents health and wellbeing as a wharenui (meeting house) with four walls. Leading Māori health advocate, Sir Mason Durie developed the model in 1984.

The wharenui walls are made up of:

Taha tinana – physical wellbeing;

Taha hinengaro – mental wellbeing;

Taha whanāu – family, community, and social wellbeing;

Taha wairua – spiritual wellbeing.

The whenua (land) forms the foundation.

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Going for a 5 aside football game every once a week
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Having dinner with my family every evening
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Listening and reading bible verses every day before sleep
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Praying every day before, during, and after study
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Walking around the grass three times week and touching the tree
